Alfajores

Ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup dulce de leche
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the egg yolks and vanilla extract to the butter mixture and mix until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing until the dough comes together.
  5. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4-inch thickness. Cut out cookies using a round cookie cutter.
  8. Place the cookies on the prepared baking sheets and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Let the cookies cool completely.
  9. Spread a teaspoon of dulce de leche on the bottom side of one cookie and sandwich it with another cookie. Repeat with the remaining cookies.
  10. Dust the assembled alfajores with powdered sugar. Enjoy these delightful South American shortbread cookies filled with dulce de leche!
